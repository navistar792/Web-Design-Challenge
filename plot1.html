City Latitude vs. Maximum Temperature

Latitude vs. Temperature

This plot shows the effect of latitude, or distance from the equator. On the X axis, find the equator (0 degrees latitude). Moving to the right, you can see that the maximum temperature gradually decreases as the cities are farther from the equator.

Notice around 50-60 degrees latitude, there are two outliers -- cities with higher than normal max temperatures. Those are Aleksandro-Nevskiy and Khomutovo in Eastern Russia. It isn't immediately apparent why these two northern cities would have higher than normal temperatures.

The southern hemisphere has the same trend -- lower temperatures for cities farther from the equator. Aside from the temperature trend, we can also learn about the distribution of cities/population in the southern hemisphere. Compared to the north, notice that there are very few cities south of -50 degrees. In general, it's also apparent that there are fewer cities in the southern hemisphere. This isn't surprising, since the surface of the southern hemisphere is 80.9% water, but 60.7% water for the northern hemisphere.1
